# Accredify — Market Research

## The Problem in Numbers

- Tamil Nadu has **~2,400 private engineering colleges** affiliated to Anna University or deemed universities
- In 2025, Anna University flagged **141 colleges** with deficiency notices — most for documentation failures, not academic failures
- **15 colleges had zero student intake** in 2025 due to non-compliance
- Anna University discovered **1,000+ fake ("ghost") faculty** in 2024-25, triggering the strictest-ever 2026-27 affiliation guidelines
- NAAC accreditation is held by fewer than 40% of Indian engineering colleges — most avoid it because the documentation burden is too high

## Competitor Analysis

### Direct Competitors

#### Kalvisalai (Peace Soft Technologies, Tirunelveli)
- **Type:** Tamil Nadu-focused college ERP
- **Employees:** ~9
- **Revenue est.:** ₹1–5 Crore/year
- **Modules:** 16+ including admissions, fees, library, HR
- **Weakness:** Broad ERP — not inspection-focused. No readiness tracking. HODs still compile inspection reports manually.
- **Price:** Not publicly listed; estimated ₹50k–₹2L/year

#### Camu Digital Campus (Chennai, est. 2013)
- **Type:** Premium cloud ERP
- **Notable:** Gartner Magic Quadrant 2026 Niche Player
- **Target:** Large universities and colleges with IT budgets
- **Weakness:** Too expensive for small-to-mid private colleges. Not inspection-body specific.
- **Price:** ₹3L–₹10L/year+ (estimated)

#### MasterSoft ERP
- **Type:** Pan-India cloud ERP
- **Notable:** Has accreditation module
- **Weakness:** Accreditation module is generic — not mapped to specific inspection body requirements. Requires significant setup.
- **Price:** ₹2L–₹5L/year (estimated)

#### Kramah Software
- **Type:** Accreditation-focused SaaS
- **Notable:** AI-powered SSR/SAR/AQAR generation; most feature-rich in this space
- **Weakness:** Premium pricing. Complexity. Aimed at large NAAC-pursuing institutions, not everyday operational use.
- **Price:** Premium tier; estimated ₹1L+/year

#### Edhitch
- **Type:** Software + mentorship combo for NAAC
- **Weakness:** Only NAAC. Not multi-body. Not a daily-use tool.

### Indirect Competitors

- **Excel + WhatsApp** — the current "system" for 90% of colleges. Free but painful.
- **Google Sheets + Drive** — used by more tech-savvy departments. No structure.
- **University ERP portals** — Anna University's own portal (AUCOE, etc.) — data entry only, no compliance tracking or reminders.

## Customer Discovery Insights

*(From observations at a private engineering college in Chennai, 2026)*

- **HODs spend 15–20 hours/week** on administrative tasks directly related to inspection compliance (normal weeks; 40+ hours in inspection months)
- The most common sentence before inspections: *"I have the data somewhere, I just need to find it and put it in the right format"*
- Faculty resistance to new tools is high — but attendance marking on a phone is already familiar behavior (many colleges use WhatsApp or basic apps)
- The trigger that makes a college buy software: **being flagged on a deficiency notice**. Colleges that received AU deficiency notices in 2025 are actively looking for solutions.
- Principals make the buying decision; HODs are the champions; faculty are the daily users
- Key objection: *"We already have an ERP"* — counter: Accredify is not an ERP replacement; it sits on top and specifically handles inspection compliance and daily academic ops

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Tamil Nadu Direct Sales (Year 1)

**Target:** Private engineering colleges that received AU deficiency notices in 2025 or are due for NAAC/NBA inspection in 2026–27

**Channel:**
1. Father-in-law network → warm introductions to HODs and principals
2. Engineering college faculty associations and workshops
3. LinkedIn outreach to IQAC coordinators and HODs
4. Presence at Tamil Nadu Private Engineering Colleges Association (TNPECA) events

**Offer:**
- First 3 colleges: 6 months free, full setup support
- Subsequent pilots: 3 months free with annual commitment
- Demo: Open the HTML prototype in a browser — no setup, no login, no pitch deck needed

### Phase 2: Word of Mouth + Referrals (Year 2)

- Each paying college refers 2–3 peer colleges (tight community — HODs know each other)
- Case study: "How [College X] went from 68% to 94% inspection readiness in 4 months"
- Partner with NAAC/NBA consultants who work with multiple colleges

### Phase 3: Platform Expansion (Year 3)

- Expand to all private degree colleges (Arts, Science, Commerce — also face inspection burden)
- Multi-college trust dashboard (management companies that run 3–10 colleges)
- All-India expansion starting with states with similar inspection structures (Maharashtra, Karnataka, AP)
